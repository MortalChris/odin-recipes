Dipping Ramen recipe

Tsukemen is cold ramen noodles served together with a hot dipping broth containing plenty of ingredients. Tsukemen has become much popular in Japan these days. The tangy soup is very rich and tasty.

Ingredients

    10-12 oz fresh ramen noodles (You can also use 6.3 oz or 180 g dry ramen noodles; For gluten-free, use these GF ramen noodles)
    ½ lb sliced pork belly
    ½ inch ginger
    3 cloves garlic
    2 shiitake mushrooms
    1.8 oz shimeji mushrooms (½ package)
    2 green onions/scallions
    1 Tbsp sesame oil (roasted)
    1 Tbsp doubanjiang (spicy chili bean sauce/broad bean paste)
    0.18 oz katsuobushi (dried bonito flakes) (1 package of this katsuobushi)

Seasonings

    ⅓ cup mentsuyu/tsuyu (noodle soup base) (to make Homemade Mentsuyu, click here.)
    1 cup water
    1 tsp miso (I use awase miso)
    1 tsp soy sauce
    1 ½ Tbsp rice vinegar

For Toppings

    6 shrimp (can be more or less)
    1 Tbsp sake (for boiling shrimp)
    2 soft/hard-boiled egg
    4 slices narutomaki (fish cakes)
    ½ sheet nori (seaweed) (cut in half)
